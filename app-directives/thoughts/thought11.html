Your Day Plan

Does your day control you or do you control your day?

A calendar is one of the oldest and most powerful tools that you can use to help with the fight against your day controlling you.

We recommend that you fill in your following daily calendar at the end of each working day during your summary session. Put in your most important tasks and events.

As you fill in your calendar leave spaces or block out “me” time. Time that you need to complete the your most important tasks.

For example: If you are a morning person you may block out the time from 9-11am for a “meeting” or some other tag to reserve that time for yourself.

Action:This evening fill in your calendar for tomorrow. Blocking out 1 time slot for yourself to get your most important thing done. Record your results in your spiral notebook.
