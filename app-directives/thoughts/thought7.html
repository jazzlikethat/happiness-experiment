Gratitude and Appreciation

Gratitude is the holy grail of positive psychology and well-being. Why?

First a definition:The quality of being thankful; readiness to show appreciation for and to return kindness.

Synonyms: Gratefulness, thankfulness, appreciation, indebtedness

Some expanded Definitions and quotes:

"A felt sense of wonder, thankfulness, and appreciation for life ." Robert Emmonds

"Gratitude is wonder; It is appreciation; It is looking back at the bright side of a set back; it is fathoming abundance; it is thanking someone in your life; it is thanking God; it is counting blessings; it is savoring; it is not taking things for granted; it is coping; It is present oriented. Gratitude is an antidote to negative emotions." Sonja Lyubomirski

"Gratitude is an affirmation of goodness. We affirm that there are good things in the world, gifts and benefits w've received. Gratitude helps us recognize that the sources of this goodness are outside of ourselves." Robert Emmons

Appreciating the grandeur of everyday life. Demonstrating appreciation to others for what I have and how they have helped me.

"The struggle ends when the gratitude begins." Amelia Boone

Learning: Gratitude helps us appreciate what we have, creating in us a renewed sense of wonder. Gratitude helps us be kind. Gratitude helps us more deeply connect with our world and the people in our lives. Gratitude shifts our focus from inside to outside. Gratitude reinforces positivity and optimism. With deeper connections to the world and those around us we are able to struggle better.

Tip:There are lots of different ways to practice gratitude that are easy and inexpensive to do. Some of our favorites are:

  • Keep a gratitude journal
  • Write a thank you letter
  • Say thank you, say it in a way that deeply connects with the other person and transfers energy.

Action:Practice expressing gratitude and appreciation. Experiment with different methods. Write your observations in your spiral notebook.
